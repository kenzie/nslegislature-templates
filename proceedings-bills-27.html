Bill No. 27
An Act to Amend Chapter 7 of the Acts of 1996, the Occupational Health and Safety Act *

Introduced by:
Hon. Marilyn More
Minister of Labour and Workforce Development

First ReadingOctober 2, 2009
Second Reading DebatesOctober 20, 2009
Second Reading PassedOctober 20, 2009

October 29, 2009
Committee of the Whole HouseNovember 2, 2009

Royal AssentNovember 5, 2009
CommencementProclamation except statutes 11, 13, 14 and 16 to 22 has effect on and after January 1, 2009; statutes 27, 28 and 29 have effect on and after April 1, 2009; statute 10 has effect on and after June 1, 2009; statute 31 has effect on and after June 23, 2009; statute 30 has effect on and after July 5, 2009; statute 9 has effect on and after September 15, 2009; statutes 24, 25, 26, 32, 33 and 34 have effect on and after November 1, 2009; statutes 2 and 3 have effect on and after December 1, 2009; statutes 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 23 have effect on and after January 1, 2010; statute 35 has effect on and after June 30, 2010
2009 Statutes, Chapter 5View PDF

Explanatory Note

This Bill streamlines the appeal process for discriminatory action decisions. The amendments eliminate an initial appeal to the Director of Occupation Health and Safety and send appeals directly to the Occupational Health and Safety Appeal Panel.

Public Involvement

Members of the public can attend meetings of these Committees and make presentations respecting any Bill when it reaches this stage. Anyone who wishes to make a presentation should let the Legislative Counsel's Office know so that their names can be listed on the agenda.

Progress of Bills Explained

The Progress of Bills is produced by the Nova Scotia Legislative Library. It tracks the Bills as they go through various stages in the House of Assembly.

First Reading is the date on which the Bill was introduced by the MLA.

Second Reading is when the house voted to give the Bill 2nd Reading after being debated in the house.

An Asterisk* following the title of the Bill indicates that the Bill has been amended between First and Third readings.

Royal Assent is the day on which the Lieutenant Governor signs the final version of the Bill and the Bill is then referred to as an Act.

Commencement is the day on which the Act takes effect.
